In front of the two shrines was a green stone where the sacrificial victims would have their hearts removed. The shrines were towards the back of the platform when viewed from the top of the stairs and were roofed with thatch. The shrine of Tlaloc was painted white and blue and that of Huitzilopochtli was decorated with carved skulls painted white on a red background. In each of the shrines were statues of the gods (called idols). These statues have not survived but from descriptions written by the Spanish conquerors we can get an idea about what they looked like.
