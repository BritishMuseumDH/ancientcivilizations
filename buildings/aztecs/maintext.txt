&tex=Many religions use special buildings in which to hold ceremonies and rituals as part of their religious worship. For example, in the Islamic religion mosques are built; in Judaism people build synagogues. In the Aztec religion, pyramid shaped temples were built for the worship of a variety of gods and to represent the Aztec view of their universe, which they thought was divided into many levels.
The Great Temple of the Aztecs stood in the middle of the capital city, Tenochtitlan. This temple was the most important structure in the city where the two most important gods were worshipped: Tlaloc and Huitzilopochtli. Tlaloc was the god of rain and Huitzilopochtli was the supreme god of the Aztecs and was linked with the sun and fire. The combination of water and fire referred to warfare. The Great Temple was the focus for huge religious ceremonies that would sometimes involve human <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/s/sacrifice.html','glos')">sacrifice</a></u></font>. Whilst the temple is no longer standing, descriptions of it written by the Spanish conquerors provide evidence of what the temple originally looked like and how it was used in Aztec religious <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/r/ritual.html','glos')">rituals</a></u></font>. Recent excavations now provide archaeological evidence that agrees with many written descriptions of the Great Temple.
