From the writings of the Spanish conquerors, we think that some of them saw inside one of the shrines on top of the Great Temple. It seems that they went into the shrine of Huitzilopochtli, the supreme Aztec god. The shrine was very dark, creating an atmosphere of mystery and making a detailed description of what was inside rather difficult. However, they describe two altars, one to Huitzilopochtli, the other to the god Tezcatlipoca. The eyes of the idols were shining stones. Huitzilopochtli carried a golden bow and arrows and wore gold and silver objects around his neck. Braziers stood in front of the idols and held the hearts of sacrificial victims. There was a strong smell and the beating of a drum could be heard. The priests that attended these shrines were dressed in black and had hair that was uncombed and knotted. As well as using the hearts of sacrificial victims, the priests often made offerings of their own blood, which they took from their earlobes.
