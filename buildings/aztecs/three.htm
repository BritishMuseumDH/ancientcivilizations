The temple was in several stories to form a stepped pyramid. The surface was covered with stucco, which is a type of plaster, and then painted. It was further decorated by large sculptures that included sculptures of serpent heads. Built into the walls of the temple were over one hundred sacrificial deposits or offerings contained in stone boxes. The offerings included objects that came from the natural world: the sky, earth and water, as the Aztecs believed that every part of their world was sacred. Most of these offerings were linked to the god Tlaloc. At the base of the pyramid, before the flight of steps that led to the top of the temple, was a stone that showed the goddess Coyolxauhqui, who was the sister of Huitzilopochtli, and had been killed by him at his birth and flung down a mountain.
