This picture shows the pyramid shaped Great Temple in a walled sacred area surrounded by other shrines and administration buildings. The first temple built on this site dates to AD 1325 but the temple was enlarged on six occasions to commemorate different rulers. Each time a ruler enlarged the temple the earlier structure was simply encased by the new structure. The pyramid structure of the temple is thought by experts to recreate the sacred hill of Coatepec where the Aztec god, Huitzilopochtli, was born. Also, for the Aztecs, the pyramid shape was like a man-made mountain, mirroring the sacred mountains that surrounded their city.
