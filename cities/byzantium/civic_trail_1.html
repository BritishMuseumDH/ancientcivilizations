The Forum

There were in fact two forums in Constantinople, but this became the main one after the city became Constantine's capital in AD 330. The forum was a public square where the market was held and other business. This example was built by Constantine and was oval in shape rather than the usual rectangular layout. It contained a tall column with a statue of Constantine on the top. At the western end was a famous statue of Athena taken from Athens. At the north end was another senate house and at the south was a large fountain. It was surrounded by a two storey colonnade filled with shops.
