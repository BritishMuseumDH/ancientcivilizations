Church of St. Polyeuktos

This church was built towards the end of Emperor Justin's reign by a very wealthy woman called Anicia Juliana. It was built to contain the relics of a martyr. This was the largest church in Constantinople until Justinian built Hagia Sophia and he was very envious of the amount of money she was able to spend on it. The domed basilica shape of Hagia Sophia is based on the structure of this earlier church.
