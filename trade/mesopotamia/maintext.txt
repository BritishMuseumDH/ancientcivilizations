&tex=The land of Mesopotamia was rich in many ways, however, it did not have many natural resources. Thus, the people of Mesopotamia needed to trade their goods with other peoples in order to acquire the goods they needed to survive.<BR><BR>Traders travelled far and wide exchanging basic goods such as grain and metals as well as luxury goods such as pearls at trading centres throughout the ancient world. The goods were weighed and measured and held different values in different marketplaces depending on local supplies and the rarity of the goods. Sometimes these goods were exchanged and sometimes they were paid for with precious metals.  Through learning about the different methods of exchange and values given to goods, it is possible to learn about the value of these goods in the ancient world.
