&tex=The Minoan civilization traded with overseas countries from earliest times. Crete was in a good position to take advantage of the trade routes around the eastern Mediterranean and as the Minoan trade network expanded in the <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/a/aegean.html','glos')">Aegean</a></u></font> they developed <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/c/colonies.html','glos')">colonies</a></u></font>, including one on the island of Rhodes and one at Miletus on the coast of <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/a/asia_minor.html','glos')">Asia Minor</a></u></font>. This favourable position brought them into contact with other civilizations around the Mediterranean, including the <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/c/canaanites.html','glos')">Canaanites</a></u></font>, Egyptians and <font color="#FFCC66"><u><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/m/mycenaeans.html','glos')">Mycenaeans</a></u></font>. The Minoans had close links with Egypt, particularly in the period between 1600 and 1400 BC. Exports from Crete to Egypt probably included textiles, grain, wood and perhaps wine and oil. Also exported was pottery and this is important to archaeologists as they can still dig up this evidence of trade.
