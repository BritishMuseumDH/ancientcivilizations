The lintels you are about to explore were placed above three doorways which led into a temple structure at Yaxchilan, so that anyone entering the building would have walked beneath them. We cannot be certain what this particular temple looked like; therefore our model is based on the standard layout of most of the main temples in Yaxchilan. These lintels were commissioned by Lord Bird Jaguar, who used them to emphasize that he was the rightful heir to the throne. They record him taking a captive and also the birth of his son, both important acts before he could become king. One lintel also shows Bird Jaguar's connection with an important ancestor, giving him a legitimate right to be king.
