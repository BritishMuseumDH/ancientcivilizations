tex=Writing allows people to communicate ideas and beliefs that are important. For the rulers of a group of people, writing can also allow them to emphasize the basis of their power to their <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/s/subject.html','glos')"><u>subjects</u></a></font>.  However, pictures can sometimes be a more powerful way of communicating with people.<BR><BR>The Maya civilization developed <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/h/hieroglyphic.html','glos')"><u>hieroglyphic </u></a></font> writing which was used to record important details, such as the names of rulers and dates of important events. These were often carved on stone <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/l/lintel.html','glos')"><u>lintels</u></a></font> and <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/s/stela.html','glos')"><u>stelae</u></a></font>. Pictures frequently accompanied the writing. The lintels were built into the structure of temples, palaces and tombs. In examples from <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/y/yaxchilan.html','glos')"><u>Yaxchilan</u></a></font>, these lintels show two rulers carrying out <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/r/ritual.html','glos')"><u>rituals</u></a></font> connected with their <font color="#FFCC66"><a href="javascript:openGlos('glossary/a/accession.html','glos')"><u>accession</u></a></font> to the throne and achievements in war.
